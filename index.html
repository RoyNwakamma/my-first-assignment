Here we are, well into year three of the covid-19 pandemic, and now we are having an outbreak of monkeypox? Is this a new virus? How worried should we be? While new information will continue to come in, here are answers to several common questions.

What is monkeypox?

Monkeypox is an infection caused by a virus in the same family as smallpox. It causes a similar(though usually less severe)illness and is most common in Central and Western Africa. It was first discovered in research monkeys more than half a century ago. Certain squirrels and rats found in Africa are among other animals that harbor this virus. Currently, an outbreak is spreading fast outside of Africa. The virus has been reported in atleast a dozen countries, including the U.S., Canada, and Israel and in Europe. As of this writing, Reuters reports more than 100 confirmed cases, making this the largest known outbreak outside of Africa. So far no deaths have been reported. Naturally, news about an unfamiliar virus spreading quickly internationally reminds us of the start of the COVID-19 pandemic. But monkeypox is not new-it was first discovered in 1958-and several features make it likely to be far less dangerous.
